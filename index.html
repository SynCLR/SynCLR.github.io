SynCLR: A Synthetic Contrastive Learning Approach for Multi-domain Speech Representation

Abstract: Achieving automated auditory perception requires the learning of effective representations, and contrastive learning has seen a resurgence by learning to be invariant to multiple augmented views in recent years. However, previous augmentation methods and resulting domain-invariant representation could perform poor generalization of representation encoding network. This paper presents SynCLR, a synthetic contrastive learning framework for generalizable speech representation. We adopt a novel diffusion based synthetic data manipulation model for view generation and guarantee a diverse speech distribution. SynCLR learns to capture varying and invariant factors for speech representations by constructing separate embedding spaces, each of which is sensitive to a single view. We demonstrate that our synthetic diffusion data manipulated method achieves state-of-the-art in terms of quality and speed. By combining these findings, we are able to considerably outperform previous methods for self-supervised learning on speaker verification and supervised learning on speech to image translation.
